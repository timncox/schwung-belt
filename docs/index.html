Belt tunes your voice as you sing and puts backing singers behind you — scale-aware pitch correction from invisible to full robot, four harmony voices that follow your key, a doubler for instant thickness, and a formant knob that makes you deeper or brighter without touching the pitch. Sing into the Move's mic, line-in, or USB-C. The Move plays the band.

Act 02 · Correction

From invisible to robot

Belt tracks your pitch ~172 times a second, decides the note you mean in the key and scale you set, and glides you onto it. Four knobs shape how honest that correction is:

YOUR VOICE→ PITCH TRACK→ SCALE QUANTIZE→ CORRECTED LEAD

Retune is the speed of the pull. At 0 the correction is instant — the T-Pain / Cher effect, pitch snapping between notes with no glide. Around 25–40 it reads as a great vocal day; high values only nudge long notes.

Amount is how far toward the target you're pulled. 100 lands on the note; 50 splits the difference; 0 is bypass — handy with harmonies: keep the lead natural, let the backup singers be perfect.

Flex (Shift+knob 2) protects expression: singing between notes — slides, scoops, blue notes — gets left alone, and correction only engages as you commit to a note. Auto-Tune calls this Flex-Tune.

Humanize (Shift+knob 1) splits your pitch into a slow line and the fast wobble on top. Correction moves the slow line; humanize keeps your vibrato riding it instead of ironing it flat. It also loosens the harmony voices — see below.

HARD is a real parameter (Perform page / Shift+knob 5): switch it on and Belt corrects instantly at full depth — your Retune and Amount knobs are never touched, so switching off lands exactly where you were. Three ways to fire it: the Perform page anywhere knobs work (including Master FX), the editor pad (row 3, column 1 — tap latches, hold is momentary), and MIDI control note 0 from a clip or keyboard — a real momentary punch you can sequence.

Act 03 · Harmonies

Backing singers, in key

Four harmony voices ride your corrected pitch at intervals measured in scale degrees — a "+3rd" is a major or minor third as the key demands, the way real backing singers hear it. Each voice gets its own small detune, a slow drift, and a place in the stereo field, so four voices read as four people, not one robot choir.

Pads 1–4 on the bottom row toggle the voices. Shift+pad steps a voice through the intervals:

Harm Level (knob 5) is the shared voice volume; Spread (Shift+knob 3) fans them across the stereo field; Humanize loosens their tuning and timing drift. Harmonies duck automatically when you stop singing pitched material — backing singers don't harmonize your consonants.

Played harmony (MIDI)

With MIDI Notes: Harmony (the default), any notes arriving at Belt's track — Move's pads in melodic or drum layout, a sequenced clip, or an external keyboard — become the harmony: hold a chord while you sing and the voices sing those exact notes as formant-preserved copies of your voice, velocity setting their level (Vel Sens). Release and the voices fall back to their interval settings. Voices hold their chord tones as the chord changes — no note-stealing wobble — and the sustain pedal works.

The sequencer trick: put the chord progression in a clip on Belt In's track. Clips keep playing under an overtake, so while Mark owns the entire surface your harmonies still change on the grid, hands-free.

MIDI Notes: Target flips the roles — the held note becomes the note you are corrected to, regardless of scale. Play the melody, sing approximately, land exactly.

Control notes (the C-2 octave, far below any music): note 0 = momentary HARD, note 1 = momentary full doubler, note 2 = momentary harmony mute — sequenceable performance gestures.

Scales

Chromatic · Major · Minor · Harmonic Minor · Dorian · Mixolydian · Major Pentatonic · Minor Pentatonic · Blues. Chromatic corrects to the nearest semitone (any key, maximum robot); pentatonics are very forgiving live — there are no wrong notes nearby.

Doubler

Knob 6 brings in two extra copies of your corrected voice, detuned a few cents apart and panned hard left and right — the "stacked takes" thickness on every chorus you've ever loved. It costs nothing to leave the harmonies off and just double.

Formant moves the resonance of your voice without moving the pitch: down is bigger and deeper, up is brighter and smaller. Subtle amounts (±20) change your apparent register; extremes are an effect all by themselves.

Act 06 · Feedback guard

The mic and the speaker

Singing into the Move's mic while its own speaker plays you back is a feedback loop waiting to happen. When Belt In sees speaker on + no line-out cable, it mutes its output and tells you why. Plug in headphones or a line out and it un-mutes by itself.

The Monitor pad (row 4, column 6) is the manual override: green = output on, red = muted. Press it to force monitoring on when you know the room can take it — the guard won't fight you until the jack state changes again.

Act 07 · Help & accessibility

Help on the device, help for everyone

On-device help

Both modules ship a full manual in the Move's own Help viewer — overview, quick start, every knob and pad, feedback-guard notes and tips. No phone required on stage: schwung's Help menu → Belt / Belt In.

Screen reader

With schwung's screen reader enabled, Belt speaks everything it does: knob changes announce full parameter names and values ("Retune speed, 25 percent" — never abbreviations), harmony pads announce the voice and interval ("Voice 1, third up"), the HARD pad, key changes from the tuner strip, and feedback-guard events ("Feedback risk. Output muted…") are all spoken as they happen.

This site

This manual is a single self-contained page built to be readable by everyone: semantic landmarks and headings for screen readers, a skip-to-content link, real buttons with visible keyboard focus, the interactive pad-map readout announced via a live region, reduced motion respected (the marquee stops, reveals don't animate), and color never used as the only signal — every pad and step also says what it is in text.

Found something that doesn't work with your setup? Open an issue on the repo.

Act 08 · Install

Two minutes to first note

Requirements: an Ableton Move with schwung installed.

Via schwung-manager: open move.local → Install Custom Module → install by GitHub repo:

timncox/schwung-belt-in — live vocals build
timncox/schwung-belt — chain / Master FX build

Or by tarball: grab belt-in-module.tar.gz / belt-module.tar.gz from the releases page and upload them in schwung-manager.

Updating: after an update, fully exit and relaunch the module so the Move loads the new files. Check the version under Help → Modules.

Act 09 · Specs

Under the hood

One pitch analysis feeds every voice: a YIN detector tracks you ~172× a second, a train of pitch marks follows your voice, and all seven output voices — corrected lead, four harmonies, two doubler streams — are PSOLA grain streams reading the same marks. That's why the whole choir costs barely more than one voice. Formant is grain-content resampling, independent of the pitch ratio.

Pitch range
85 Hz – 1 kHz (F2 and up)
Engine latency
~26 ms, dry path delay-matched
Voices
lead + 4 harmonies + 2 doubler
Scales
9 · keys C…B
Audio
44.1 kHz, stereo, 128-frame blocks
Presets
all settings; monitor state never saved
